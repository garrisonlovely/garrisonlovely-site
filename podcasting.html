Selected episodes

Episode 35

Yoshua Bengio on Why AI Labs are "Playing Dice with Humanity's Future"

Bengio is the second-most cited living scientist and one of the so-called "Godfathers of deep learning." He and the other Godfathers — Hinton and LeCun — shared the 2018 Turing Award, computing's Nobel prize. In November he was commissioned to lead the first "State of the Science" report on the capabilities and risks of frontier AI.

Episode 32

Rutger Bregman on Why People Are Decent, Effective Altruism, and Causing Tucker Carlson's Meltdown

Bregman is the bestselling author of Utopia for Realists and Humankind: a Hopeful History. He has been profiled in the New York Times and interviewed on the Daily Show. Rupert Murdoch has been spotted reading his book, and Tucker Carlson called him a "fucking moron."

Episode 8

Emily Bazelon on Prosecutors, Jeffrey Epstein, and Kamala Harris

Bazelon is a staff writer at the New York Times Magazine and the Truman Capote Fellow at Yale Law School. She is the author of Charged: the New Movement to Transform American Prosecution and End Mass Incarceration, and co-hosts Slate's Political Gabfest.
